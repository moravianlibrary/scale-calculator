On antique maps´ scale

The application is a tool for quick determination of the scale on antique maps. It is suitable for setting the scale, which was put into historical formats using both historical units of measurement and the metric system, in the currently used format 1:M.

The scale on antique maps, both manuscript and printed, is usually put in two ways: no figure at all or so called graphic scale (a line segment). The application enables setting of the scale in both cases. It is intended to help librarians and archivists building catalogues and databases, historical cartographers and laymen interested in antique maps. It is not intended to substitute carto-metric analyses when using advanced tools used in digital cartography. That is why the calculation is approximate and it is necessary to round the gained figures.

The following unites of measurement used in Central Europe in the Early Modern Era are taken in the account:

  • Bohemian units of length used until 1765,
  • Moravian ones used until 1758,
  • Silesian ones used until 1750,
  • Prussian ones used in the 18th century,
  • Slovak-Hungarian ones (18th and 19th centuries),
  • Lower Austrian system introduced since the mid-18th century in the above mentioned countries
  • metric system which was introduced in Austro-Hungarian Empire with the 1871-Act, which came into force 1. 1. 1876.
